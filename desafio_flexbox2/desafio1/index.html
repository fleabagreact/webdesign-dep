Getaway Car - Taylor Swift

About

A getaway car is typically used in movies for criminals to escape from the scene post-crime. This is a reference to the infamous criminal couple Bonnie and Clyde. On May 23, 1934, after a long history of criminal activity, Bonnie and Clyde were ambushed and killed by police as they tried to flee in a 1934 Ford Deluxe.

Taylor draws from the story of Bonnie and Clyde to warn her lover in this line. Since their love started in a “getaway car” – as a rebound to get out of her previous relationship – they were doomed from the start of their relationship.

“Sirens” helps bring the image of a crime scene to life, with the love Taylor receives from her new man being a constant reminder of an improper act she committed. In this case, Taylor used her new man as the “getaway car” to get away from a stale, possibly unhealthy, relationship. However, given the nature of how it started, it was highly unlikely that their love would last.

Many have speculated that this song is most likely a reference to Taylor’s romance with actor Tom Hiddleston, who she met at the 2016 Met Gala, while she was still publicly dating DJ Calvin Harris. This reinstates the idea that Tom served as a “getaway car” for her to break it off with Calvin.

The song comes full circle, and just as Taylor left Calvin for Tom in a getaway car, it seems that she has now done the same to Tom – eventually leaving him for someone else.

She cries as she gets away, evidencing the fact that they both are hurt by the situation– but she nonetheless leaves.
